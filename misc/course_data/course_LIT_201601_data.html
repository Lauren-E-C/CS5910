Literacy in Content Areas - 18793 - LIT 396 - HY2
ADO Students (teacher candidates) enrolled in LIT 396/Hybrid during Spring 2016 with Dr. Walsh will participate in a unique opportunity that offers them a high school practicum experience in their content area and a practical tutoring experience, in addition to their 3 credit LIT396 Literacy and Learning in the Content Areas course. All components will be held on-site at PV Moore High School in Central Square on Wednesday afternoons. Teacher candidates will be assigned to a high school classroom for weekly practicum (observation and TA-type assistance), and they will also be assigned at least one high school reader/writer to tutor using the best literacy practices and approaches learned in the college classroom. Dr. Walsh will provide specific tutoring instruction and support in order for the teacher candidate to be able to implement and document individualized instruction in the high school setting. Our hope is that the teacher candidate may return for another field placement in the same classroom, but that arrangement has not been worked out yet. Tentative Schedule for Wednesdays at PV Moore HS: · NOTE: Car pooling must be arranged prior to January 27th so that everyone is in attendance on time each Wednesday. A back-up plan/ride is also recommended. · NOTE: Dr. Walsh will be in contact with all students registered for this LIT396 section via Bb email the week prior to the start of the semester. The course syllabus with the schedule of classes and expectations for the semester will also be posted on Bb the week prior to the start of the semester. · NOTE: Online Requirement: During several weeks of the semester, teacher candidates will complete an online assignment to bring into the face to face class. · NOTE: If a few schedules do not permit the Wednesday practicum, teacher candidates may participate in the tutoring and the course without participating in the PV Moore practicum on Wednesdays. They may be placed at PV Moore HS for practicum on a different day that fits into their schedule, but will still need to arrive by 2:00 for the 2:15 tutoring. 12:45-2:15- Practicum Placement in the high school classroom. This is the last block of instruction for the day. Teacher candidates will observe, take notes on what they see, assist the teacher much like a TA might, including offering assistance to individual students. 2:15-3:00- Tutor a high school student in reading and writing in the PV Moore HS Library with oversight by Dr. Walsh 3:00-5:00 LIT396 class meets in the PV Moore HS Library. (Exact dismissal time depends on hybrid schedule. We will always dismiss by 5pm.)
